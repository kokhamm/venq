Could VENQ.ai fully replace community managers?

Currently, VENQ.ai can effectively handle 100% of low-level community manager responsibilities. But to answer the question directly, we will never know the extent of the limits of Conversational AI capabilities; they reach as high as the sky. So, maybe it will allow us to fully replace community managers one day. Or not.  While using VENQ.ai just a bit of human attention is required — a community lead or marketing director, for example, can configure and provide ongoing support to the chatbot. You are just going to help it to operate effectively. As chatbot algorithms require time to learn and improve, having a dedicated person to monitor responses and assist in their development is crucial in the beginning. As the chatbot becomes more proficient, it will only require approximately 30 minutes of daily support.

What to do if I need to receive turnkey community management support?

If you find that SaaS alone does not really cover your needs, and you desire a greater level of delegation, feel free to reach out to us. VENQ.ai is the result of our experience in community building and community management for Web3, which helps us tackle any complex challenges you may encounter.

It was founded by two companies: Solus Foundation, which supports the growth of Web3 projects through investment raising and marketing, and InSoft Partners, which ensures project development. For more details about us, please refer to our pitch deck.

How long does it take to set up VENQ.ai?

If you already have a knowledge base and a community history that includes a number of accurate answers from community management, the process will typically take around 5–10 minutes. But if you want a more customized and optimized experience, the time investment will depend on your willingness to dedicate. Fortunately, the initial setup is incredibly easy and quick.

How often should the bot be updated?

The AI bot doesn't require any upgrades unless you want it to develop. However, if you have new information, updates, or engage in new activities (if not, it's worth questioning why), then regular chatbot training will be necessary. The bot will respond based on what you teach it, so ensure that you provide all the web3 community moderation updates for accurate answers.

Is VENQ.ai better than a quality community management?

We, as humans, believe that Saas should not be considered more important than human work. However, we hope you will carefully read our landing page and understand the advantages of VENQ.ai compared to other options.  Our main goal is not to replace community managers, but to give them the opportunity to use their unique abilities, which even the most powerful AI cannot replicate. One reason we created VENQ.ai is because community managers often have to do tasks that can and should be automated. So, to clarify once again: this is not a competition to determine what or who is better - robots or humans - but rather a focus on making community management endeavours more efficient.
